Prosperity 4 is IMC's online trading challenge designed for university students. The challenge takes players on a journey through deep space, where it's up to them to develop a trading strategy in a virtual simulated market.

Throughout 5 rounds consisting of 1 algorithmic challenge and 1 manual challenge, players are presented with unique trading opportunities that require skill, adaptability, and drive to succeed. With more success comes more XIRECs (the currency in Prosperity), a growing outpost, and the opportunity to climb to the top of the leaderboard. Prosperity is your chance to see how your skills stack up against other students across the globe while learning more about trading in a gamified environment that is competitive, collaborative, and fun.

The official Prosperity challenge takes place between April 14th — April 30th. The schedule is:

Tutorial Round (available to participants immediately after signing up): March 16th - April 13th

Round 1: April 14 - 17

Round 2: April 17 - 20

Intermission: April 20 - 24

Round 3: April 24 - 26

Round 4: April 26- 28

Round 5: April 28 - 30

  1. Sign up and create your account

  2. Join a team or start your own — there can be up to 5 players per team.

  3. Connect with the Prosperity community on Discord

  4. Brush up on your trading terminology & Python skills with our e-learning center via the Prosperity Wiki

  5. Use the tutorial round to practice programming your first Python program

  6. Start counting down to Round 1 on April 14th!

There are no hard pre-requisites for coding or trading knowledge to participate. Basic Python & trading knowledge will help a lot, but you can find lots of resources to get started in our Wiki. This includes concepts such as: what bid/ask prices mean, how order books work, basic market making concepts, and position management. The competition rewards both technical implementation and trading intuition.

Come in with a positive & curious mindset (a bit of a competitive edge doesn't hurt!), and you'll still have a good time and learn a lot in the process.

While Prosperity is designed for university students, the platform is open to anyone to sign up and participate for learning purposes. However, eligibility for leaderboard ranking, prizes, and recognition is subject to the following conditions:

Top 25 Teams must meet the following requirements:

  • Proof that they were enrolled at a university at the time of registration, and are at least 18 years of age, upon request.
  • Teams that include any participant who placed in the Top 10 of a previous edition of IMC Prosperity will be removed from the rankings.
  • If any member of a team fails to meet the eligibility requirements, the entire team may be disqualified at IMC's sole discretion.

Top 5 Teams and the Manual Trading Winner must additionally:

  • Make themselves available for a verification call with IMC to confirm their genuine and active participation in the challenge.
  • Eligibility for prizes, and recognition is limited to participants resident in EMEA, North America, South America, India, Australia, or Hong Kong at the time of registration. Participants resident outside these regions may participate in the challenge and appear on the leaderboard, but are not eligible to receive prizes

Team composition is open to changes during the first two rounds. After Round 2, team composition is locked and participants should therefore ensure all team members meet the eligibility requirements before this point.

The following are grounds for disqualification from Prosperity entirely:

  • Ineligible affiliation: including current or accepted employment at IMC, its affiliates, or competitor firms engaged in similar trading activities.
  • Multiple team participation: a participant may not be a member of more than one team.
  • Legal, reputational, or compliance concerns: including submission of false documentation, exploitation of platform bugs, plagiarism of another participant's code or algorithms, or any conduct that may violate applicable laws or harm IMC's reputation.

All participants are required to review and accept the full Terms & Conditions prior to registering for Prosperity.

Prosperity is a team-based virtual trading simulation made up of 5 trading rounds. Each trading round consists of 1 algorithmic challenge where each team must upload a Python program and 1 manual challenge where each team submits their answers directly into the platform. All round submissions are evaluated by a trading engine at the end of each round. The algorithmic results do not influence the manual results and vice versa; they are independent of each other. The results of each team's PnL after each round are then recorded in the leaderboard so each team can observe their position.
