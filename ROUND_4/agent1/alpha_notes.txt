Own-trades / market-trades handling
-----------------------------------
- Local backtester behavior: state.own_trades and state.market_trades are
  disjoint. Do not assume market_trades contains our fills.
- Production decision:
  - HYDROGEL_PACK uses market_trades + own_trades union because our fills expose
    named counterparties and HYPER-style HYDRO improves materially.
  - VELVETFRUIT_EXTRACT stays market_trades only because including own fills was
    already known to add noise.

Accepted alpha
--------------
1. HYDRO named-bot union tilt
   - Pattern: Mark 14 / Mark 38 / Mark 22 HYDRO bot flow is useful when public
     prints and our own fills are unioned.
   - Evidence: HYDRO PnL is 144,059.0 with HYPER/HYDRO12 vs 137,648.0 in
     BOTVELVET. This adds 6,411 total PnL on the same option stack.
   - Products: HYDROGEL_PACK.
   - Implementation: accepted in trader_AGENT1_HYDRO12.py and promoted through
     trader_AGENT1_BEST.py.
   - Risk: medium. The alpha relies on live own_trade counterparty names matching
     the website. The diagnostic probe trader exists to test this.

2. Keep BOTVELVET's VELVET public-tape behavior
   - Pattern: BOTVELVET's VELVET handling beats HYPER's VELVET calibration.
   - Evidence: VELVET PnL is 96,300.0 in BOTVELVET/HYDRO12 vs 95,173.0 in HYPER.
   - Products: VELVETFRUIT_EXTRACT.
   - Implementation: accepted by inheriting BOTVELVET and replacing only HYDRO.
   - Risk: low-medium. This is still named-bot alpha, but it is stable across all
     public days and improves day 2 without hurting day 3.

3. Wing bot overlay into VEV_5200
   - Pattern: wing prints in VEV_5300/5400/5500 from Mark 22 / Mark 01 carry a
     small directional signal into VEV_5200.
   - Evidence: agent2 variant A and agent1 HYDRO12_WING5200 both add +165.0 PnL,
     all from VEV_5200 day 3: 85,168.0 vs 85,003.0 for HYDRO12/HYPER.
   - Products: VEV_5200, using VEV_5300/5400/5500 bot flow.
   - Implementation: accepted in trader_AGENT1_HYDRO12_WING5200.py and promoted
     in trader_AGENT1_BEST.py.
   - Risk: high-ish for size, because the edge is tiny and sparse. Kept because
     it improves min day and does not change other products.

Rejected or not promoted
------------------------
1. VELVET tight-spread bullish tilt
   - Pattern: data-only markout is very strong. VELVET spread <= 1 had n=186,
     +1.874 mid-to-mid at 100ms, 91.4% hit. Spread <= 2 had n=1,218, +1.248 at
     100ms, 81.5% hit.
   - Evidence against implementation: crossing/tilting on it hurt VELVET badly.
     trader_AGENT1_HYDRO12_TIGHT.py ranked 779,461.5; combo ranked 779,626.5.
   - Products: VELVETFRUIT_EXTRACT.
   - Decision: rejected for production. Keep as research only.
   - Risk: spread/exit cost eats the apparent markout; also correlated with the
     bot alpha already being used.

2. HYDRO Mark 38 / Mark 22 pair boost
   - Pattern: public pair table showed rare HYDRO Mark 38 vs Mark 22 prints with
     raw next-tick moves around 3 points.
   - Evidence: trader_AGENT1_HYDRO12_PAIR.py tied HYDRO12 exactly at 786,244.5;
     no realized improvement.
   - Products: HYDROGEL_PACK.
   - Decision: not promoted.
   - Risk: too sparse and already mostly absorbed by the union HYDRO signal.

3. Basket cluster to VELVET
   - Pattern: multi-leg voucher clusters are visible, often Mark 22 selling
     basket-like tails.
   - Evidence: symbols>=4 Mark22 clusters had n=291 but VELVET d300 mean only
     +0.029 and hit_up 45.0%.
   - Products: VELVET and VEV wings.
   - Decision: rejected as direct VELVET signal.
   - Risk: visually tempting but not directionally stable.
