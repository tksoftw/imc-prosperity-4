What survived
-------------

1. HYDRO raw-mid fair

   Raw top-of-book mid beat wall-mid for HYDRO in this setup.
   Kept the historical anchor around 9991, fast/slow EMA blend, and smaller
   take size from the old smile_surface-style hydro.

   Result:

     HYDROGEL_PACK: 97696.0 vs 97114.0
     Gain: +582.0

2. ATM residual one-tick mean reversion

   This was the main new edge.

   Signal:

     residual_move = option_mid_change - delta * velvet_mid_change

   If the option jumps more than the underlying explains, fade that jump.
   This directly exploits the one-tick option quote artifact / negative
   autocorrelation from the hedgehogs writeup and our ROUND_3 data.

   Kept strengths:

     VEV_5000: delta 0.90, strength 1.20
     VEV_5100: BS delta, strength 0.75
     VEV_5200: BS delta, strength 0.75

   Result:

     VEV_5000: 12038.5 vs 8949.5
     VEV_5100:  5119.0 vs 4340.5
     VEV_5200:  2541.5 vs 2092.5

3. Bigger VEV_5000 sizing only

   VEV_5000 accepted more size well, probably because the residual fade has
   more room and better fill quality there.

   Kept:

     take size 12
     quote size 8

   Rejected:

     take size 16 / quote size 10, because it reduced total PnL.

4. Small wing residual fade on VEV_5400 / VEV_5500

   The same one-tick residual fade was tested on wings.

   Kept:

     VEV_5400 delta 0.20, strength 0.60
     VEV_5500 delta 0.08, strength 0.50

   Real gain appeared on VEV_5400:

     VEV_5400: 7311.0 vs 7067.5
     Gain: +243.5

   VEV_5500 stayed flat but did not hurt.

5. VEV_4000 tape tilt into VELVET fair

   VEV_4000 bot trades had weak directional information for future VELVET.
   Added a small temporary tilt to VELVET fair and ATM spot edge.

   Kept:

     +1.0 * v4_dir

   Note:

     +1.5 tested about the same, so kept +1.0 for less live-risk.

What died
---------

1. Basket bounce / stale basket overlay

   Offline data showed basket events had positive next-tick option drift,
   especially around VEV_5200 and VEV_5300. In backtest execution, acting
   after observing the basket was too late or got bad queue/fill behavior.

   Verdict:

     Rejected.

2. Far-wing paid orders on VEV_6000 / VEV_6500

   These books are basically always:

     bid 0, ask 1

   Paying 1 cannot be sold profitably later in the data. Posting bid 0 is
   harmless but usually sits behind existing queue, so it gets no fills.

   Verdict:

     Keep zero-bid absorber only; do not pay 1.

3. Top-book imbalance tilt

   Data said bid/ask volume imbalance predicted next mid move. Backtest said
   the execution got picked off, especially on VELVET, VEV_5100, and VEV_5000.

   Verdict:

     Rejected.

4. Wall-mid pull

   Data said wall_mid - top_mid predicted next tick. Backtest said not
   tradable after spread/queue costs.

   Verdict:

     Rejected.

5. ATM fly / vertical spread reversion

   The notebook confirmed strong negative autocorrelation in ATM vertical
   spread changes. Direct fair-value nudges using fly or 5100/5200 vertical
   residuals looked tempting but reduced or barely matched PnL in execution.

   Verdict:

     Rejected from final experiment.

6. Bigger VEV_5100 / VEV_5200 sizing

   Scaling both to size 8 increased trades but reduced PnL, especially on
   VEV_5100. Keep their original nimble size 6.

   Verdict:

     Rejected.
