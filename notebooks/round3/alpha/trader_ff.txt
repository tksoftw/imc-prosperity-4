1. WHY trader_ff EXISTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Real-deploy logs in imc_logs/ROUND_3/ revealed that backtest PnL is not
a reliable predictor of real PnL. Specifically:

  * trader_final_OPTIMUM had the LOWEST backtest day-2 of our top
    candidates ($63K) but the HIGHEST real day-2 ($14,042).
  * trader_MADSCIENTIST had high backtest day-2 ($86K) but real
    day-2 was only $11,550 because soft HYDRO skew BLED $4K real.

So trader_ff is built per-product: each strike's strategy is whichever
ancestor scored highest *on REAL data*, not whichever scored highest in
backtest. Then I cherry-pick a few proven backtest improvements that
don't conflict with the real evidence (residual-fade on 5000, basket
absorber on 5500).

5. KEY DECISIONS (and why they survived this round)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

DON'T soften HYDRO skew.
  * MADSCIENTIST.log: skew 0.005 -> HYDRO real $3,735.
  * optimum.log:     skew 0.050 -> HYDRO real $7,868.
  * Bot flow on HYDRO real-deploy is DIFFERENT from backtest. Aggressive
    quotes get adversely-selected on real but make money in backtest.

DO soften everything else (VELVET / 5300 / 5400 / 5000 / 5100 / 5200).
  * MADSCIENTIST.log proved soft skew on these adds real PnL.
  * 5300 alone went $695 -> $1,380 real with soft skew.

KEEP basket absorber + zero-bid wings.
  * Costs nothing if wing seller doesn't bite.
  * Adds $200/day when they do.

KEEP residual fade on 5000.
  * EXPERIMENTS.log: 5000 real $108 (vs OPTIMUM $-70).
  * Backtest also confirms big edge ($30K vs $2K on 5000).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. CAVEMAN LESSONS LEARNED THIS ROUND
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1) BACKTEST PnL ≠ REAL PnL. The bot mix on real day 2 differs from
   the historical CSV. Things that win in backtest can lose real, and
   vice versa. ALWAYS compare per-product real outcomes when you have
   them, not just the totals.

2) SOFT SKEW = EDGE EVERYWHERE EXCEPT HYDRO. HYDRO's quote depth is
   thin so soft skew exposes us to adverse selection on real. Every
   other product benefits.

3) CHEAP OPTIONALITY IS FREE. The basket-active absorber on 5500 and
   the 0-bid wing scoops cost nothing when they don't fire. Keep them
   in until you have evidence they bleed.

4) PRIORITIZE PROVEN REAL WINNERS PER PRODUCT. The whole architecture
   is just "pick the best ancestor per strike + tweak a few knobs that
   the backtest agrees on." Beats trying to globally optimize.
