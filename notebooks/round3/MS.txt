2. WHAT WORKED (in order of $$ contribution)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

(a) ULTRA-SOFT POSITION SKEW                              ≈ +$70K
    The single biggest finding. Both trader_final and OPTIMUM used
    aggressive position skew (0.05-0.06 ticks per lot of inventory).
    The intent: cut the offer when long, lift the bid when short, to
    encourage mean-reverting inventory.

    Reality: the bots in this game are mostly UNINFORMED takers per
    notebooks/round3/trader_identification.ipynb. There's no adverse
    selection to skew against. Aggressive skew was just CUTTING our
    quotes below where the book was willing to pay, leaking alpha.

    Tested grid:
        HYDRO skew:    0.05 → 0.03  → 0.015 → 0.005 → 0.0
        HYDRO PnL:    97K → 113K → 124K → 139K → 137K   (0.005 wins)

    Final values: HYDRO 0.005, VELVET 0.005, ATM smile 0.03,
    ext-anchor 5000/5500 0.025, 5300/5400 0.025-0.03.

(b) POSITION LIMITS NEAR 300                              ≈ +$15K
    Game cap on every voucher is 300 (per the user's spec). Both
    trader_final and OPTIMUM used soft caps in the 50-200 range to
    avoid blowups. Bumping the caps to 290 on the high-volume strikes
    (4000/4500/5200/5300) and 200 elsewhere unlocked size for the
    naturally-positive MM strategies.

(c) HYDRO ANCHOR 9991 (vs claude_carry_2's 9980)          ≈ +$3K
    Quick grid search (9980 → 9988 → 9990 → 9991 → 9994) confirmed
    9991 is optimal on this dataset, matching trader_final.

(d) WING-BASKET ATM TILT                                  ≈ +$0-1K
    See section 3.A — alpha is real but mostly absorbed by MM.

3. WHAT WAS DISCOVERED VIA explore_alphas.py / simulate_alpha.py
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

A. WING-SELLER BASKET → ATM DRIFT (validated, t-stat ~22)

   When the Wing Seller dumps ≥3 OTM strikes at the same tick:
       VEV_5200 mid drifts  +0.875 ticks @ 100   (SE 0.04, n=280)
       VEV_5300 mid drifts  +0.345 ticks @ 100   (SE 0.04)
       VEV_5400 mid drifts  +0.077 ticks @ 100   (SE 0.02, fades fast)

   VELVET itself does NOT drift (-0.02, noise) — so this is a pure
   IV-surface effect, not a stale spot price. Likely: the wing-seller
   dump compresses OTM extrinsic, and the smile recalibrates upward
   on the ATM strikes via the surface fitter.

   PROFITABILITY:
   Lift ASK on basket fire, exit at NEW ASK after 100 ticks:
                          avg PnL   total
       VEV_5200 (≥3):     +1.78    +$507
       VEV_5300 (≥3):     +0.67    +$188
       VEV_5400 (≥3):     +0.17    +$48

   Lift ASK, exit at BID (cross-spread):
       VEV_5200:          -1.11    -$314    ← SPREAD KILLS ALPHA
       VEV_5300:          -1.40    -$394
       VEV_5400:          -1.22    -$346

   Conclusion: the alpha is real but the cross-spread cost (~3 ticks)
   exceeds the drift (~0.87 ticks). Direct take is unprofitable.
   Soft fair-value tilt added but mostly absorbed by the MM that's
   already accumulating long via wider quotes.

B. VERTICAL SPREAD MEAN-REVERSION (validated stat, fails P&L)

   Spread = mid_K - mid_{K+100} mean-reverts strongly:
       5100-5200:  μ=71.26  σ=3.62  diff_AR(1) = -0.42
       5200-5300:  μ=48.79  σ=3.75  diff_AR(1) = -0.40
       5300-5400:  μ=30.81  σ=3.51  diff_AR(1) = -0.37

   But round-trip cost = sum of bid/ask spreads on both legs ≈ 5-7
   ticks. Even at the 1.5σ entry threshold (5.6 ticks of mid spread
   deviation), expected reversion barely covers cost. Tested in code,
   bled $13K. Disabled.

C. VEV_4000 TRADER COPY (signal not extractable)

   Per trader_identification.ipynb, the VEV_4000 trader has a 53.4%
   hit rate at 5k horizon (mildly informed, sizes 1-3). My
   explore_alphas.py confirms +0.41 tick VELVET drift @ 1k after
   their BUY prints (SE 0.21, t≈2).

   Tried: VELVET fair tilt of ±1.0 when v4 BUY/SELL active.
   Result: VELVET PnL unchanged. Either the rust backtester reports
   trade events differently than the historical CSV (possible — the
   wing_dump signal works fine), or the per-tick fire rate is too
   low to materially shift our MM. Skipped.

D. VELVET ACCUMULATOR INSIDER COPY (already in trader_final)

   The Accumulator (≥9 lot VELVET prints) drives +1.78 tick drift @
   500 with t-stat ~9. Per-product analysis showed:
       VELVET drifts +1.78  (used)
       VEV_5200 drift +0.05 (noise — already priced in)
       VEV_5300 drift -0.11 (noise)
   So leveraging via options doesn't add anything — the spot tilt

6. CAVEMAN LESSONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  STAT-SIG ALPHA ≠ PROFITABLE ALPHA.
    Wing-basket drift = t-stat 22. Vertical spread = strong mean-rev.
    Both have positive expectations on PAPER. Both BLEED in P&L when
    you account for the 3-tick spread cost of crossing a thin book.

  SKEW EATS MORE THAN IT SAVES (when bots are uninformed).
    Five rounds of optimization on the previous traders couldn't move
    HYDRO past $97K. The skew knob — set to a "sensible" 0.05 by every
    prior author — was leaving $42K on the table. Always question your
    risk-management assumptions when you trade against uninformed flow.

  POSITION LIMITS ARE NOT RISK LIMITS.
    Game cap = 300 on every voucher. Soft caps at 50-200 (in earlier
    traders) were "robustness" parameters but were really just choking
    PnL on naturally-mean-reverting positions.
