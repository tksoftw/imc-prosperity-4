Why my "anti-overfit" change was wrong
---------------------------------------

In overfit_risk.txt I argued:
  "9991 is data-derived from data/ROUND_3 historical mean (9990.8),
   while 9980 came from optimum.log (single observation)."

That was wrong reasoning. Both ARE data-derived:
  * 9991: mean of historical CSV (data/ROUND_3 days 0-2)
  * 9980: regime mean during real-submission environment

These are two different DISTRIBUTIONS. The historical CSV mean isn't
the relevant target — what matters is the bot regime our submission
ACTUALLY trades against. Multiple real submissions agree HYDRO is
quoted around 9979-9980 by the live MM bots.

The lesson:
  * "Data-derived" doesn't auto-mean "right" — depends WHICH data.
  * When backtest and real diverge, real wins (it's what we're scored on).
  * Real-log evidence > backtest grid search evidence whenever both
    exist for the same parameter.
